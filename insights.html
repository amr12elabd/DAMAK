Preparing Your Soil Before the Spring Growth Phase

Soil Management●8 min read●DAMAK Agronomy Team

Soil structure, drainage, and organic matter levels at planting time set the ceiling for everything else that follows in a growing season. No amount of well-timed fertiliser or growth regulator application will fully compensate for a poorly prepared seedbed — yet soil preparation is the step that most small-scale farmers compress or skip entirely when they're under time or budget pressure.

This article outlines the key parameters to assess before your spring crops go in — and the practical, low-cost steps you can take to address deficiencies before they affect your yield potential.

Understanding Plant Growth Regulators: A Farmer's Guide

PGRs are among the most powerful tools available to modern growers — but they're also among the most misunderstood. Used correctly, they can improve fruit set, prevent lodging, accelerate rooting, and extend shelf life. Used incorrectly, they can stress the plant or produce no measurable effect. We explain the basics: what PGRs are, how they work at the cellular level, and what to expect at each growth stage.

Managing Heat Stress in Summer Crops: What Actually Works

Egypt's summer temperatures push many crops beyond their optimal thermal range during the critical flowering and fruit-set period. Heat stress reduces pollen viability, accelerates respiration, and depletes the sugars available for fruit development. Post-harvest quality — shelf life, appearance, sugar content, firmness — is largely determined by what happens in the two to three weeks before harvest, not after it. Calcium levels, potassium balance, and cell-wall integrity at harvest time are all influenced by decisions made during the growth phase.
